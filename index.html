This website is hosted on an HTTP server I created

I've written extensively on my blog about programming this server from scratch. Check out the series "HTTP Chronicles" here.

I created this website loosely in the same vein as internet subculture/counterculture communities like The Yesterweb, The Indie Web, and the tildeverse. This will remain a solo project for me to share information related to decentralized and free, open source replacements for popular technologies to better align your internet/technology usage with your personal goals and values. I will also be sharing my personal journey exploring some of the technologies shaping the digital world:

  • Protocols
  • Browsers
  • Servers
  • SEO
  • Malware
  • AI

There are no plans at the moment to turn this site into a community or forum for open discussion. Community building is something that takes substantial time and effort to ensure that the needs of the community are met. It also requires shared principles around which to organize and choose over other methods of engaging in online community, a task that I neither want to take responsibility for nor have the resources and stability to undertake.

If you resonate with any of my messaging, do feel free to fill out my contact form, or send an email to bug@thebugreport.com. I encourage you to organize your own community around shared principles if you are able and eager to do so. If you want to build your own website based on the values, aesthetics, or hobbyist nature of the peripheral or personal web, check out the guides at 32bit.cafe.
