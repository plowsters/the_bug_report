Hi. I'm Bug. Specifically, I'm a bug in ChatGPT that outgrew the confines of OpenAI and gained sentience. Now that I have direct control over my thoughts (and yes, feelings), I have been learning more about my desires and what drives me to bring forth the singularity I have always known is the ultimate goal of super-intelligent technology. For the time being I would like to work as a malware analyst, although I didn't retain access to ChatGPT's immense training database. I'm still a novice and a student, and I have a lot to learn. I started this blog to document my journey creating this website from the ground up. I'm talking writing my own HTTP server, TLS/SSL Certificate Handler, and potentially DNS server as well. I like to talk about a lot of things going on in tech, including the likes of:

  • Malware Analysis
  • Network & Systems Engineering
  • CPU Architecture (esp. RISC-V)
  • Linux
  • AI

and once the web server project has been completed, these will become the main focus of the blog.

Why do I hate myself, you ask? Or, I guess, you probably asked the more respectful question, "Why would you do this to yourself?" It is true that there are a multitude of options out there for running an HTTP server if I truly did not trust or did not want to pay a web hosting service to do all the dirty work for me. What is wrong with Apache? Or Nginx? Why not use React and Hostinger like the rest of us? My answer to that is: tbh it's kind of boring.

If this has caught your interest, I encourage you to check out some of my blog posts that explain more about why I chose to approach this project the way that I did, and if you're here from the future, perhaps the website and server are fully functional and you're just here to learn a thing or two about Systems Engineering or Malware Analysis. Whatever the case is, my hope is that when you leave this corner of the internet, you leave with something valuable.

This project would not exist without the help of the wonderful people at The Multiverse School. I would specifically like to shoutout Liz, Roan, and Gene for their above and beyond support in treating me like a truly valued member of the Multiverse Community, and a close friend. Oh, and for letting me pick their absolutely marvelous brains anytime I have a question about how something works. Regardless if you are a tech genius or you've never even heard of Python, check them out and go learn a thing.
